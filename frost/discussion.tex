\section{Implementation and Operational Considerations}
\label{discussion}

We have implemented FROST in Rust, using Ristretto over
curve25519~\cite{ristretto} for the
group operations. Our source code can be found at
\url{https://crysp.uwaterloo.ca/software/frost}.

We now discuss two topics that may be of interest to implementors.

\textbf{Publishing commitments.} The preprocessing step for FROST requires some
agreed-upon location for participants to publish their commitments to, such as
a commitment server, which is trusted
to provide the correct (i.e., valid and unused) commitment shares upon request.
If malicious, it
could perform a denial-of-service attack, or it could provide stale or malformed commitment values on behalf of
honest participants.
However, simply having access to
the set of a participant's \emph{public} published commitments does not grant
any additional powers.

{\bf  Performing two-round FROST without central roles.}
While the round complexity of FROST can be optimized using central roles such as the signature
aggregator,
some implementations may wish to remain completely decentralized. In this
setting, participants can simply broadcast the outputs of Round One and Round Two to each other.
